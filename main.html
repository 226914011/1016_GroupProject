The Internet of Things


Introduction

The Internet of Things -- IoT -- through its broadest definition, is a network of physical devices that encompasses any object or "thing" that can be linked to the Internet, ranging from factory machines and equipment, sensors, and cars to mobile devices such as smartphones and smartwatches, and even home appliances that generate and share data.

Network Layer

Wireless

  • RFID

    1. Low frequency RFID
      • 30-300 KHz
      • Read range: up to 10cm
      • Pros: perform better in the presence of metal/ liquids
      • Cons: short read range, slower data read range

    2. High frequency RFID
      • 3-30 MHz
      • Read range: 10cm-1m
      • electronic ticketing/payment/data transfer
      • used in NFC: payment cards, hotel key card

    3. Ultra-high frequency RFID
      • 300MHz-3GHz
      • Read range: up to 12m
      • Pros: faster data transfer rates
      • cons: more sensitive to interference from metals/ liquids/ electromagnetic signals

    4. Battery-Assisted Passive (BAP) RFID
      • hybrid type/semi-passive
      • power source -- ensure all captured energy from the reader is used to reflect the signal
      • x own transmitters

  • Bluetooth mesh networking

    • It is a computer mesh networking standard based on Bluetooth Low Energy. It allows communication of devices over Bluetooth radio. Bluetooth 4.0 and Bluetooth 5.0 are the most commonly used Bluetooth version currently.

    Bluetooth 4.0 Bluetooth 5.0
    Range 60m up to 1.5km
    Speed 25Mbit/s 50Mbit/s
    Date transfer rate 1Mb/s 2Mb/s

    Advantages:
    • easy to set up the network
    • allows many-to-many device communication
    • wide adoption of Bluetooth (many smart devices support Bluetooth)
    • low power consumption

    Disadvantages:
    • expensive to implement
    • complicated topologies
    • difficult to maintain network
    • easy to have redundant connections

  • WiFi

    • It is a technology for the local area networking, which is based on wireless internet protocol. Devices can connect through a shared access point or connect individual devices directly by radio waves.


    For IEEE 802.11ah standard:

    Range: up to 1000m

    Frequency: sub-gigahertz band

    Data transfer rate: 78 Mbps (16 MHz in Sub-1 GHz)

    Power saving: Traffic Indication Map (TIM), Delivery TIM (DTIM)


    Advantages:
    • flexible than wired network
    • easy to set up and connect
    • low energy consumption (down to 100 milliwatts)
    • large coverage area (up to 1000m)
    • high data rate (up to 347 Mbps)

    IoT applications: autonomous lightning, smart security, smart home thermostats


  • Z-wave

    • Z-Wave is a wireless networking protocol for home automation. It's a mesh network that uses low-energy radio waves to connect devices.


    Range 100meters
    Frequency 908.42 MHz
    Data rate 9600 bits/s and 40 kbits/s

    Advantages:
    • Installation is simple
    • No additional cabling is needed for installation
    • The wireless signal is unaffected by other devices
    • Efficiencies in energy

    Disadvantages:
    • Distance of signal transmission is short
    • Number of Limited nodes is 232

  • 5G

    • It is the fifth generation technology standard for cellular networks. Devices are connected by radio waves through a local antenna. 5G networks can operate on up to three frequency bands including low, medium and high.


    Frequency:

    • Low-band 5G: 600–850 MHz
    • Mid-band 5G: 2.5–3.7 GHz
    • High-band 5G: 25–39 GHz

    Download speed:

    • Low-band 5G: 30–250Mbit/s
    • Mid-band 5G: 100–900 Mbit/s
    • High-band 5G: around 1 Gbit/s

    Latency: down to 1ms

    Range: depends on the frequency being used, can cover couple hundred kilometers


    Advantages:
    • Greater bandwidth
    • Higher download speeds
    • Low latency
    • Can connect a large number of devices

    Disadvantages:
    • It is still under process
    • High cost for infrastructure
    • Many old devices are not compatible to 5G
    • Lack of technological support in many places

  • LPWAN

    • A low-power broad-area network (LPWAN) is a wireless telecommunications wide area network that allows long-range communications at a low bit rate. It is designed for connecting business or users.


    Range 2 km to 1,000 km
    Frequency 100 Hz
    Data transmission speed 0.3 kbit/s and 50 kbit/s

    Advantages:
    • Low power usage
    • Batteries are cost-effective
    • Long-distance

    Disadvantages:
    • Delay problem is serious
    • Low data rate
    • High latency

Application Layer

  • Definition

    • The application layer is in charge of supplying the user with application-specific services. It specifies uses for the Internet of Things, including smart houses, smart cities, and smart health.

    The third layer is the application layer. The aim of the application layer is to bring IoT social needs and industrial technologies together.

  • MQTT

    • Message Stream Telemetry Transport: MQTT is a TCP-based publish/subscribe protocol. It was structured primarily as a client/server protocol by IBM. The clients are publishers/subscribers, and the server functions as a TCP broker to which the clients bind. A subject may be published or subscribed to my clients. This contact is done by the broker, whose task is to arrange subscriptions to authenticate the customer for security purposes.

  • Application

  1. Near Field Communication (NFC) Payment

    2. In the retail market, NFC is one of the fastest-growing Internet of Things applications. Customers who use NFC payment do not need to pay with cash or a card; instead, they may make contactless payments with their NFC-enabled smartphones. It limits the amount of time it takes to make a payment while still increasing certainty. On the vendor's side, all that is needed for NFC payment is an NFC payment device.

  2. Remote Control Appliances

    3. Many different home appliances can now be remotely controlled. Home appliances have been Wifi-enabled, ranging from light bulbs to refrigerators to fans and televisions. You can use this to monitor different functions in your home without ever leaving your bed.
